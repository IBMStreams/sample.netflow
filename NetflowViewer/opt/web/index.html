IBM Netflow Viewer

The IBM Netflow Viewer uses IBM Streams to illustrate the data flowing in and out of the IBM T.J.Watson Research center in real time. Network routers throughout the building continuously feed Netflow summaries of their traffic to Streams. Streams locates the source and destination address of each flow with geographical data obtained from MaxMind and OpenStreetMap, aggregates the flows by city, and sends the aggregations to your browser in real time through a WebSocket. The aggregated flows are overlaid on maps and satellite imagery provided by OpenStreetMap, Esri, and NASA. IBM offices and labs are drawn in blue; Internet websites are drawn in green. This application is anonymous: it does not decode, store, or display any information about the sources or destinations of any data flowing through the lab.
